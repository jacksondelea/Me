Networks
"What if the internet was a city? What would be its roads, buildings, and parks?” This question was levied at the 2018 Decentralized Web Summit, where programmers, urban planners and theorists collaborated to explore “decentralized forms of networking through the lenses of cities, urbanism, and architecture.”
Urban metaphors have laid at the heart of “cyberspace” imaginaries since its inception, but it's clear that now, the city of the internet is one we no longer want to live in. In a moment dubbed by some to be the turning point into “post-neoliberalism,” new paradigms of digital networking are emerging in reaction to the current state of internet infrastructure and governance. This essay traces the evolution of the internet through a series of urban spatial metaphors, from the birth of Network Culture to the new cyberpunk futures of decentralized computing. I hope to offer new frameworks and tools for the analysis of new forms of networking to come.
